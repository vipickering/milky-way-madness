Tasks and Activities

Mapping soley the tasks grouped by activies (in a similar fashion to an activity map) across a Milky Way Map can aid in understanding what services are doing in the real world.

When to use this mapping technique

Milky Way Maps can be quite intimidating for non-technical people. Or those stakeholders who are not deep in to the details of a service (or services).

Starting with something simple, familiar and easy to grasp helps to anchor peoples minds on to concepts and better understand what they are interpreting.

This approach is especially useful when using a user centred approach to desiging services :

(the) Activities people do -> Require enterprise capabilities -> (that) Manifest themselves as functional actions -> (which) Require assets to be delivered.

Giving you the opportunitly to layer one concept at a time and use the value-chain as your common anchor between concepts

When this approach is helpful

  • Dealing with stakeholders who want to broadly understand a service(s) or enterprise core activities
  • Layering concepts for non-technical people
  • Focusing on user-centred design aspects, journey mapping, usability and accessibility
